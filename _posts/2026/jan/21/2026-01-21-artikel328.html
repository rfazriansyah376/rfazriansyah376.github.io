Pre-Launch Week 0: The Foundation Setup (Days 1-7)

Time Commitment: 2-3 hours total. Do this before you create any public content.

Day 1: Clarify Your Core (60-90 mins)

Task: Complete the Personal Brand Blueprint (Template from Article 7).
Action Steps:
1. Open a new document.
2. Answer: My Why (Mission), My How (3-5 Core Values), My Who (Ideal Audience Avatar).
3. Define your 3 Content Pillars (topics you'll own).
4. Describe your Brand Voice (3 adjectives).
Done When: You have a 1-page document summarizing your brand's heart.

Day 2: Audit & Cleanup (30-45 mins)

Task: Audit your primary social profile.
Action Steps:
1. Update your bio to reflect your new blueprint (clear who you help and how).
2. Update your profile picture (professional, clear, friendly).
3. Update your link (to a landing page, link-in-bio tool, or your best content).
4. Archive or delete any glaringly off-brand old posts.
Done When: Your profile clearly communicates your new direction to a first-time visitor.

Day 3: System Setup (45-60 mins)

Task: Set up your content and tracking systems.
Action Steps:
1. Create a Content Calendar (Use Google Sheets/Notion. Columns: Date, Platform, Topic, Status).
2. Create a Weekly Metrics Tracker (Columns: Date, Platform, Content, Comments, Saves, Shares).
3. Bookmark the Resource Hub (Article 7) and Series Index (Article 8).
Done When: You have two empty but structured spreadsheets/documents ready to use.

Day 4: Tool Stack (30 mins)

Task: Choose and set up one core tool.
Action Steps:
1. Sign up for Canva (free tier is fine). Create a simple "Brand Kit" with your 2 primary colors and a font.
2. Decide on your primary content format (writing, video, audio). Choose one tool: CapCut for video, Otter.ai for transcription, Grammarly for writing.
Done When: You have Canva open with your colors saved, and one content tool installed.

Day 5: First Content Plan (60 mins)

Task: Plan your first week of content using the Core-to-Branch framework.
Action Steps:
1. Choose one Core Topic from your Pillar 1.
2. Decide on your Core Asset Format (e.g., a LinkedIn article, a 5-minute video, a long Instagram carousel).
3. Brainstorm 3 Branch Ideas (e.g., a Twitter thread summary, a TikTok tip, an email snippet).
4. Block time on your calendar to create these in the next 7 days.
Done When: Your content calendar has topics filled in for the next 7 days.

Day 6: Mindset & Accountability (20 mins)

Task: Set your intentions and find accountability.
Action Steps:
1. Write down your #1 goal for the next 30 days (e.g., "Publish 8 pieces of content," "Have 10 meaningful conversations," "Get 5 saves on a post").
2. Tell one person about your 30-day commitment.
3. Write down your biggest fear about putting yourself out there, then write a counter-mantra (e.g., Fear: "I'll look stupid." Mantra: "My genuine effort will help someone.").
Done When: You have a written goal, an accountability partner, and a mantra.

Day 7: Rest & Reflect (0 mins work)

Task: Do nothing related to your brand.
Action: Seriously. Take the day off. Go for a walk. This builds the habit of sustainability from day one.
Done When: You feel recharged for the launch tomorrow.

Month 1, Weeks 1-4: Consistency & First Content

Now, the public work begins. Focus on action, not perfection.

Weekly Rhythm (Repeat for Weeks 1, 2, 3, 4)

Monday (60-90 mins): Create Core Asset.
• Write that article, film that video, record that podcast.
• Focus on delivering one clear value.

Tuesday (45 mins): Create Branch Content.
• Create 2-3 pieces adapted from your Core Asset (e.g., social posts, graphics, email).
• Use your Canva templates.

Wednesday (30 mins): Schedule & Polish.
• Schedule your content for the week using platform native schedulers or a free tool like Later/Buffer.
• Double-check links and captions.

Thursday (Daily, 15 mins): Engage.
• Respond to any comments on your posts.
• Leave 2-3 thoughtful comments on other creators' posts in your niche.

Friday (20 mins): Review & Plan.
• Update your Metrics Tracker with this week's numbers.
• Briefly note what topic got the best response.
• Choose next week's Core Topic.

Saturday/Sunday: Rest.
• No brand work. This is non-negotiable.

Weekly Focus Themes

Week 1 Focus: Launch. Your goal is simply to hit "publish" on your first Core Asset and 2-3 branch pieces. Celebrate doing it.

Week 2 Focus: Engagement. Your goal is to spark one conversation. Ask a question in your caption. Reply to every comment with a question back.

Week 3 Focus: Value. Review your Week 1 & 2 content. Which piece provided the most obvious utility? Create a "Part 2" or a deeper dive on that subtopic.

Week 4 Focus: Review. Conduct your first Monthly Review (see Progress Tracking section below). Celebrate your consistency.

Critical "Do Not" List for Month 1

  • ❌ Do NOT check your follower count more than once a week.
  • ❌ Do NOT compare your Day 5 to someone else's Year 5.
  • ❌ Do NOT change your strategy based on one post's low likes.
  • ❌ Do NOT skip your rest days.
  • ❌ Do NOT buy followers or use engagement pods.

Your only job in Month 1 is to build the habit of creating and engaging consistently. That's a massive win.

Essential Daily & Weekly Habits

These small, repeated actions build the muscle of an authentic brand.

Daily Habits (5-15 minutes total)

  1. The Morning Input (5 mins): Instead of scrolling social media first thing, read one article related to your niche or listen to a short podcast. This fuels your mind with quality input, not comparison.
  2. Authentic Engagement (5-10 mins): Go to your notifications. Reply to comments. Then, visit the profile of one person who engaged and leave a genuine comment on their latest post. This builds real connection.
  3. Idea Capture (1 min): When a content idea hits, immediately put it in your phone's notes app or a dedicated "Idea Bank" document. Don't trust your memory.

Weekly Habits (30-60 minutes)

  1. Metrics Review (15 mins, Friday): Update your tracker. Look at Saves and Shares, not just likes. What topic or format is winning?
  2. Content Planning (30 mins, Friday): Plan next week's Core Topic and brainstorm 2 branch ideas. Put them in your calendar.
  3. One Community Action (15 mins, Any day): Do one thing to foster community. Examples:
    • Run a poll in your Stories asking what your audience struggles with.
    • Share a user's post (with credit) that aligns with your values.
    • Ask a "question of the week" in your post caption and feature the best answer in your next post.

Bi-Weekly Habit (20 mins)

Consumption Audit: Every two weeks, review who you're following. Unfollow accounts that make you feel inadequate, jealous, or distract from your niche. Follow 2-3 new people who inspire you with their authentic work.

Monthly Habit (60 mins)

The Monthly Review & Energy Check: At the end of each month, answer these three questions in a journal:
1. What content felt most like "me" to create?
2. What task drained my energy the most this month? (Find one way to simplify it next month.)
3. What was my favorite interaction with someone in my audience?
This keeps you aligned, sustainable, and connected to the human element.

2. The "Don't Break the Chain" Calendar

Print a blank calendar for the month. Each day you complete your core habit (e.g., "15 mins of engagement" or "publish scheduled content"), put a big "X" on that day. Your goal is not to break the chain of X's. This visual motivator is powerfully simple.

3. Accountability Partnerships

Find One Person: This could be a fellow creator, a friend, or in an online community. Set up a weekly 15-minute check-in (via text, voice note, or call). Share:
• One thing you shipped.
• One lesson learned.
• Your goal for next week.
This external commitment dramatically increases follow-through.

4. The "Win Jar"

Get a physical jar or a digital note. Every time you have a small win—a nice comment, a save on your post, finishing a piece of content you're proud of—write it down and put it in the jar/note. On days you feel discouraged, read a few wins. This builds evidence of progress against self-doubt.

What's Next After 30 Days?

Congratulations on completing your first 30 days! This is just the beginning. Here's how to transition from launch mode to sustainable growth.

Phase 2: The Next 60 Days (Months 2 & 3)

Focus: Deepening value and building community.
Actions:
1. Double Down on What Worked: Analyze your 30-day tracker. Which content got saves/shares? Create more of that.
2. Start an Email List: Use a free lead magnet (a checklist, a template) related to your best content to start building your owned audience.
3. Host Your First "Community Moment": Run a live Q&A, a Twitter Space, or an Instagram Live. Keep it casual and interactive.
4. Conduct Your First Quarterly Review: Use the template from Article 7/8. Reflect on your first 90 days.
