The Mediumish Jekyll Theme is visually elegant and technically efficient, making it perfect for writers and developers who want full control of their content. But to grow a loyal readership, you need consistent engagement beyond just blog visits. This is where newsletters and content automation come in. Integrating a newsletter system in Jekyll helps you notify readers of new posts automatically, nurture long-term connections, and drive repeat visits — all while keeping your website lightweight and fast.

Why Add a Newsletter to a Static Jekyll Blog

Unlike dynamic CMS platforms, Jekyll doesn’t come with built-in newsletter or email subscription tools. However, that doesn’t mean you can’t create an automated mailing system. By combining Jekyll’s static nature with external automation tools, you can deliver a modern reader experience that feels dynamic but runs entirely on static infrastructure.

Adding newsletters serves several strategic purposes:

  • Audience Retention: Keep readers returning to your blog without relying on social media algorithms.
  • Content Distribution: Automatically send updates when new posts are published.
  • Monetization: Build a subscriber list for future paid newsletters or affiliate offers.
  • Engagement Insights: Measure what kind of content resonates most with your audience through email analytics.

Simple Newsletter Integration Options

You can integrate newsletters into Mediumish using no-code or low-code tools. Here are some practical methods that maintain Jekyll’s simplicity while giving readers a smooth experience.

1. Using Mailchimp or EmailOctopus Embeds

Mailchimp, EmailOctopus, and ConvertKit offer free embeddable forms that work with static sites. You can add these directly to your Mediumish theme footer, sidebar, or after each blog post.


<div class="newsletter-box">
  <h3>Join the Newsletter</h3>
  <p>Get updates whenever I publish a new post.</p>
  <form action="https://youremailservice.us1.list-manage.com/subscribe/post?u=xxxx&id=xxxx" method="post" target="_blank">
    <input type="email" name="EMAIL" placeholder="Enter your email" required>
    <button type="submit">Subscribe</button>
  </form>
</div>

This snippet can be placed inside _includes/footer.html or as a reusable include like _includes/newsletter.html for consistency. You can then call it with:


{% raw %}
{% include newsletter.html %}
{% endraw %}

2. Integrating Static Forms with Netlify or Formspree

If you host your Jekyll blog on Netlify or use Formspree, you can easily capture emails without any backend code.


<form action="https://formspree.io/f/xxxx" method="POST">
  <input type="email" name="email" placeholder="Your email address" required>
  <button type="submit">Subscribe</button>
</form>

These tools send submissions directly to your email or connected database, allowing you to build a subscriber list without external CMS tools or heavy JavaScript libraries.

3. Substack or Revue Embeds for Hybrid Publishing

If you want to connect your blog content to an existing newsletter platform like Substack or Revue, embed their sign-up widgets in your Jekyll layouts. This hybrid approach allows you to run your blog on Jekyll but manage email delivery from a dedicated service.


<iframe src="https://substack.com/embed" width="480" height="320"></iframe>

This is an ideal setup for writers who want Jekyll’s speed but Substack’s built-in audience and analytics.

Automating Content Updates

Automation ensures that readers are instantly informed when new content goes live. Instead of manually sending emails or updates, you can link your RSS feed to automation services that handle this for you.

1. Using Zapier or Make (Integromat)

Zapier and Make can connect your Jekyll site’s RSS feed with an email marketing platform. Each time a new post is published, your subscribers receive an automatic update.


Trigger: New RSS feed item from https://yourdomain.com/feed.xml  
Action: Send campaign via Mailchimp or Gmail

This simple workflow turns your static blog into a fully automated communication channel without hosting databases or CMS systems.

2. RSS-to-Email Integration

Many newsletter platforms, including Mailchimp, have a built-in “RSS to Email” feature. Once configured, every new post triggers an automatic newsletter delivery to all subscribers. It’s a hands-free approach that ensures your content reaches readers consistently.

To use it, make sure your feed.xml file is properly set up in your Jekyll project:


plugins:
  - jekyll-feed

This plugin automatically generates an RSS feed compatible with most automation platforms.

3. Scheduled Content Previews

If you publish long-form content regularly, consider setting up a system that sends weekly digests or “what’s new” summaries. You can automate this with RSS filters that gather only selected categories or tags, such as:

  • /tag/tutorials/feed.xml
  • /tag/design/feed.xml

That way, your newsletter subscribers receive relevant updates, not every single post — improving engagement and reducing unsubscribe rates.

Styling and Branding Your Newsletter Section

Since the Mediumish Jekyll Theme already has clean typography and spacing, your newsletter section should follow the same design language. Use consistent fonts, border radii, and button styles. You can adjust this in your main CSS or SCSS partial:


.newsletter-box {
  background: #fafafa;
  border-radius: 12px;
  padding: 20px;
  text-align: center;
}
.newsletter-box input {
  border: 1px solid #ddd;
  padding: 10px;
  width: 70%;
  border-radius: 6px;
}
.newsletter-box button {
  background: #2a2a2a;
  color: white;
  border: none;
  padding: 10px 16px;
  border-radius: 6px;
  cursor: pointer;
}

Visual consistency makes your signup area blend seamlessly with the Mediumish aesthetic. It should look like a natural part of your blog, not an intrusive popup.

Testing and Tracking Engagement

Once integrated, test your setup carefully:

  • Submit test emails to confirm form functionality.
  • Check that automation triggers only once per post.
  • Use UTM parameters in links to track newsletter-driven visits in Google Analytics.

Over time, analyze open and click rates to identify which topics or headlines attract the most engagement. Use this data to refine future posts or newsletter titles.

Scaling to Advanced Automations

Once your basic setup works, you can expand to more advanced automations:

  • Segmentation: Send different emails based on reader interest (e.g., Jekyll tutorials vs design insights).
  • Welcome Sequences: Automatically send a series of emails to new subscribers introducing your best articles.
  • Automated Digests: Weekly or monthly summaries sent via Zapier or Make, pulling posts dynamically from your feed.

These steps turn your blog into a living ecosystem — where your audience is nurtured automatically, even while you focus on creating new content.

Final Thoughts: Build a Self-Sustaining Content System

Integrating newsletters and automation into the Mediumish Jekyll Theme bridges the gap between static publishing and active audience engagement. By combining tools like Mailchimp, Netlify Forms, and Zapier, you can keep your blog entirely static yet highly dynamic in communication.

Ultimately, the goal is to make your readers feel connected — informed when you publish, appreciated for subscribing, and eager to return. This sustainable system helps your site grow naturally without constant manual promotion.
